Good Enough Software

Why I Chose This Section

I chose to write about this section because it addresses a different kind of problem than I usually think about, but certainly a valid problem, not only in programming, but also in other aspects of life. I often times find myself caught up in the mindset that everything I do must be done perfectly that I spend too much time overthinking it. I don't think I realized it before reading this section, but sometimes I overthink my hitting technique in baseball, a project in electronics, and of course, in my code. This is good to an extent, but once I have these things to a level of good enough I can struggle to stop, which leads to a faulty adjustment being made. A specific example was ladt semester on my electronics project I added in an extra step to the game I was making and it drew too much current, thus ruining the project (thankfully it was easy to undo).

How to Apply What I Learned

In the previously given example, adding an extra element to my game would have enhanced the project, however, given the two week time constraint, and the fact that I had already met the project requirements, it would have been wise of me to stop there. I should not risk ruining the project right ahead of the deadline when it already exceeds the requirements. Not only would I be jeopordizing the project, but I am doing so at the expense of my own time for other projects as well as my stress levels. I hope to learn not to leave my projects in the oven too long, so to say I need to learn when is best to stop. No one wants an incomplete project, but going too far can be equally as detrimental.
